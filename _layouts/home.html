Research

Numerical methods for multiscale computation
We put forward a general synthetic iterative scheme (GSIS) for steady/transient solutions of kinetic equations and reconstructing multiscale gas/phonon transport phenomena reliably. The scheme has merits of fast convergence and asymptotic preserving, and can reduce consuming of computational resources by several orders of magnitude.
Discontinuous Galerkin method for kinetic equations/macroscopic equations
We develop optimal solution strategies for the high-order discontinuous Galerkin methods that are adopted in the kinetic/macroscopic moment solvers in the GSIS, to make the simulation tool robust and economical for practical enegineering-design simulations.
Physics of rarefied gas dynamics/non-Fourier heat conduction
We utilise the developed methods and models to enable a priori solutions of rarefied gas dynamics/non-Fourier heat conduction encountered in emerging multiscale technologies such as lab-on-chip devises, carbon neutrality, next-generation vacciunes, micro air vehicles, and high-altitude flights.
Nonequilibirum thermo-chemical ralaxation after shock
We develop sophisticated numerical methods to understand the nonequilibirum thermo-chemical relaxation that dictate the flow dynamics aournd re-entry capsules, allowing optimal designs of aerodynamic shape and heat shield.
